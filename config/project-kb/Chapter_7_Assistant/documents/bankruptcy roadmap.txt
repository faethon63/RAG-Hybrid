Bankruptcy Roadmap KB (Chapter 7 - CACB)

Phase 1: The "Worksheet" Phase (Offline Prep)
Goal: Organize all data on PDF "worksheets" so the online interview is fast and accurate.

Day 1: Financial History (Lookback)
Focus: Income history and payments to insiders.

Your Drive Files: 2024_FEDERAL_RETURN.pdf, 25 Federal Tax Return.pdf, b_107_0425...pdf.

Action: Fill out Statement of Financial Affairs (Form 107).

Question 4: List 2024 and 2025 gross income from your tax returns.

Question 6: List payments to creditors >$600 in the last 90 days.

Key Instruction: Do not list normal business expenses here; focus on personal debts and payments.

Day 2: Debts (The "Matrix")
Focus: Listing everyone you owe money to.

Your Drive Files: form_b106ef.pdf, form_b106d.pdf.

Action:

Form 106E/F: List unsecured debts (Credit cards, medical bills, personal loans).

Form 106D: List secured debts (Car loans, mortgages).

Key Instruction: You must list the mailing address for the claims center, not the payment address.

Day 3: Leases & Contracts
Focus: Ongoing contracts you want to keep or reject.

Your Drive Files: b_006g_1207f.pdf (Form 106G).

Action: List your apartment lease and any car lease.

Decision: Decide if you will "Assume" (keep) or "Reject" (end) the lease.

Day 4: Means Test Income Calculation
Focus: Calculating your 6-month average income (CMI).

Your Drive Files: Bus Aug 25.pdf through Bus Dec 25.pdf.

Action:

Sum the "Gross Receipts" from your P&L PDFs for the last 6 full months.

Divide by 6 to get your Current Monthly Income.

Download Worksheet: Form 122A-1 PDF (Save this to Drive).

Check: Is this number lower than the CA Median Income for a household of 1?

Phase 2: The "Online" Phase (eSR Portal)
Goal: Transfer your worksheet data into the official court system.

ðŸ”— Primary Link: CACB eSR Portal Note: You have 45 days to finish once you start.

Day 5: Account Setup & Initial Entry
Action: Create your eSR profile.

Input: Enter basic info (Name, SSN, Address).

Input: Transcribe data from your Form 106E/F and 106D worksheets.

Day 6: Income & Expenses
Action: Enter your financial "Snapshot."

Input:

Income: Enter the 6-month average calculated on Day 4.

Expenses: Enter your current monthly living expenses (Rent, Food, Utilities) from Form 106J worksheet.

Day 7: Assets & Exemptions
Action: List your property and protect it.

Your Drive Files: form_b106ab Assets.pdf, Schedule C_ Attachment.pdf.

Input:

Assets: Transcribe every item from Form 106A/B.

Exemptions: Select "System 1" (704) or "System 2" (703) in the portal and assign values to protect your assets.

Phase 3: The "Filing" Phase (Mandatory Locals)
Goal: Prepare the forms eSR does NOT generate. Timing: Do this immediately after submitting the eSR package.

Day 8: Download & Fill Mandatory Local Forms
The eSR system will not create these. You must download, fill, and sign them manually.

Statement of Related Cases (F 1015-2.1):

Link: Download PDF

Purpose: Discloses if you've filed for bankruptcy before.

Declaration Re: Payment Advices (F 1002-1):

Link: Download PDF

Purpose: States that you are self-employed and do not receive paystubs from an employer.

Verification of Master Mailing List (F 1007-1):

Link: Download PDF

Purpose: Confirms the list of creditors you entered is accurate.

Day 9: Final Snapshot & Review
Action: Login to eSR one last time.

Update: Check your Bank Account balance in the "Assets" section. Update it to the exact penny of your current balance.

Submit: Click "Submit" in the eSR portal.

Day 10: The "Wet Signature" Packet
After submitting via eSR, you must mail/deliver these physical pages to the court within 10 days.

eSR Declaration (Mandatory):

Link: Download PDF

Action: Print and sign this immediately after submitting online.

Statement of Social Security (Form 121):

Link: Download PDF

Filing Fee:

Prepare a Cashier's Check or Money Order for $338, OR

Fill out Form 103B (Application for Waiver) if your income is <150% of the poverty line
